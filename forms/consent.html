1. Purpose of the research
The purpose of this study is to examine what people know about words that they cannot recall.

2. Study Procedures
If you agree to participate in this study, you will study a list of words and then be asked to remember the words later during a recall test. You will be given a prompt about what the word means and then type the word if you recall it. If you indicate that you do not recall the word, you will then indicate if have any sense that it is on the tip of your tongue. You will then see four other words and select the one which you think is closest to what you were attempting to recall. Then you will be given a second opportunity to recall and type the word before the next trial. The entire study should take about 15 minutes to complete. These tasks will be conducted online in your web browser.

3. Recording devices
Your responses will be collected online using the jsPsych software platform. The program will record the recalled words that you type, your sliding-scale recollection ratings, and your button-press responses when choosing the related words.

4. Potential benefits
Participants will have the benefit of experiencing a typical cognitive psychology study. The possible benefits to society may be that this study will add to the psychological literature concerning memory retrieval and intermediate knowledge states.

5. Potential Risks
There are minimal risks associated with participating in this study.
